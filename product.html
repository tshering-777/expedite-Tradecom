Air Conditioner


A major or residential appliance, system, or mechanism known as an air conditioner changes the humidity and temperature of the air in a space.
Depending on the characteristics of the air at the moment, it may also be used to heat or chill the space.
Evaporation is occasionally employed in place of the normal refrigeration cycle, most frequently for comfort cooling in buildings and automobiles.
The term "HVAC" in construction refers to a full system of ventilation, heating, and air conditioning.
Through a window and a wall The typical single-room air conditioners have interior controls and fit in a window or wall opening.
As interior air is circulated over the evaporator by a fan, it is cooled.
A second fan pumps air over the condenser, warming it up there.
Heat is removed from the space and released onto the surrounding area during this procedure.
A big house or building might have multiple of these units, allowing for the independent cooling of each room.

Whole-house or big commercial space cooling is provided by central (ducted) air conditioning. By adding air-louver-control boxes, central (ducted) air conditioning frequently provides moderate multi-zone temperature control capacity.
